# Google — Interview Experience & Hiring Guide (2026)

## Overview
Google is a Product based company. This file documents its 2026 hiring pipeline, interview rounds, a representative candidate experience, and preparation tips. Metadata tags: Google, product-based, FAANG, big tech.

## Hiring Process
Online Assessment (2–3 medium-hard DSA problems) followed by 1–2 technical phone screens and an onsite/virtual onsite loop of 4–6 rounds mixing DSA, system design (for SDE-2+), and Googleyness/behavioral.

## Interview Rounds
1) Online Assessment. 2) Phone/video technical screens. 3) Onsite loop: DSA rounds, system design (senior levels), and a Googleyness/leadership round.

## 2026 Updates
Google has increased emphasis on Googleyness plus added a coding round with AI-autocomplete disabled to test unaided reasoning, alongside a separate round that allows AI tools to test collaborative debugging.

## Sample Candidate Experience
A candidate described one round with AI-assist tools fully disabled focused on pure algorithmic reasoning, and a separate round where they collaborated with an AI coding assistant on a debugging task.

## Preparation Tips
Practice explaining your thought process out loud, not just producing correct code. Prepare 6–8 STAR stories covering conflict, failure, leadership, and ambiguity.
