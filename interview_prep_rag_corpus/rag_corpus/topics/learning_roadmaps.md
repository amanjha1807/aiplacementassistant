# Personalized Learning Roadmap Templates — 2026

## How Roadmaps Are Personalized
tags: [roadmap, personalization]

Roadmaps are generated along two axes: track (target company type and role level) and timeline (available prep time). Below are template roadmaps; the assistant should adapt week counts and topic depth based on the user's actual timeline and current skill assessment (e.g., from a diagnostic quiz).

## Track: Fresher — Service-Based Companies (6-Week Plan)
tags: [roadmap, fresher, service-based, 6-weeks]

- Week 1–2: Aptitude (quant, logical reasoning, verbal) + core DSA basics (arrays, strings, basic sorting/searching).
- Week 3: DBMS fundamentals (normalization, joins, transactions) and OS basics (process scheduling, deadlocks, memory management).
- Week 4: OOP concepts across your chosen language + intermediate DSA (linked lists, stacks/queues, recursion).
- Week 5: Mock technical interviews focused on one live coding problem + project walkthroughs; practice explaining resume projects in under 90 seconds.
- Week 6: Full mock interview cycles (technical + HR), company-specific research, and voice-based mock practice.

## Track: Fresher — Product-Based Companies (10-Week Plan)
tags: [roadmap, fresher, product-based, 10-weeks]

- Week 1–3: Core DSA topics (arrays, strings, linked lists, stacks/queues, trees) with 3–4 problems per topic per day, Easy → Medium.
- Week 4–5: Graphs and Dynamic Programming, Medium → Hard, with emphasis on explaining approach before coding.
- Week 6: Backtracking, heaps, tries, bit manipulation; begin light system design reading (URL shortener, rate limiter).
- Week 7: Full HLD practice — one system design problem every two days.
- Week 8: Behavioral prep — build 8 STAR stories; research target company culture/values.
- Week 9: Full mock interview loops (2 DSA rounds + 1 system design + 1 behavioral), voice-based practice.
- Week 10: Weak-area review based on mock interview feedback, resume-based mock Q&A.

## Track: Experienced Professional (3–5 yrs), Lateral Hiring (8-Week Plan)
tags: [roadmap, experienced, lateral-hiring, 8-weeks]

- Week 1–2: DSA refresher focused on Medium-Hard problems across all topics — prioritize speed and clean code.
- Week 3–4: System design deep-dive — both LLD and HLD, including one AI-systems question.
- Week 5: Leadership/behavioral prep — stories demonstrating technical ownership, mentoring, and cross-team collaboration.
- Week 6: Resume-based deep dives — defend every architectural decision on every listed project.
- Week 7: Full mock loops including a bar-raiser-style round; practice negotiating scope and clarifying ambiguous requirements out loud.
- Week 8: Company-specific final review, compensation/negotiation prep, voice-based rehearsal of the full loop.

## Track: Career Switcher / Non-CS Background (12-Week Plan)
tags: [roadmap, career-switcher, non-cs, 12-weeks]

- Week 1–2: Programming fundamentals and basic problem-solving.
- Week 3–5: Core DSA from the ground up, slower pace, correctness before optimization.
- Week 6–7: DBMS and OS fundamentals for interview purposes.
- Week 8–9: Intermediate DSA (graphs, DP basics) plus first exposure to system design fundamentals.
- Week 10: Resume-building guidance — framing non-CS or bootcamp projects credibly, anticipating "why are you switching careers."
- Week 11–12: Mock interviews at service-based difficulty first, then progressively harder; heavy HR/behavioral rehearsal.

## 2-Week Crash Plan (Any Track, Interview Imminent)
tags: [roadmap, crash-plan, 2-weeks]

- Days 1–4: Highest-yield DSA patterns only — two pointers, sliding window, DFS/BFS on grids, basic DP.
- Days 5–7: One system design problem per day if targeting product-based roles.
- Days 8–10: Behavioral story-building (minimum 5 solid STAR stories) and company research.
- Days 11–13: Full mock interviews daily, voice-based rehearsal, resume Q&A prep.
- Day 14: Light review only — re-read STAR stories and project summaries; avoid learning new material.
