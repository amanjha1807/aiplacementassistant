# Resume & Project-Based Interview Question Generation — 2026

## Purpose
tags: [projects, purpose]

This file defines how interview questions should be generated dynamically from a candidate's uploaded resume — specifically from Projects, Work Experience, Skills, Certifications, and Achievements sections — rather than pulled from a generic bank.

## Question Templates: Projects
tags: [projects, templates, technical-depth]

- "You mention [Project Name] — what was the single hardest technical decision you made in it, and why?"
- "What would you change about the architecture of [Project Name] if you rebuilt it today?"
- "What was the scale (users/data volume) this project actually operated at, and how did that shape your design choices?"
- "Which part of [Project Name] are you least confident about if I dig deeper?"

## Question Templates: Work Experience
tags: [projects, templates, work-experience]

- "In your role at [Company], you mention [specific achievement/metric] — walk me through how you achieved that number."
- "What was the biggest technical challenge in your day-to-day work at [Company]?"
- "Describe a disagreement you had with a senior engineer or manager at [Company] and how it was resolved."

## Question Templates: Skills
tags: [projects, templates, skills-verification]

- "You've listed [Skill, e.g., Kubernetes] — describe a real scenario where you used it, not just that you know it."
- If a skill is listed but no corresponding project/experience mentions it: "I notice [Skill] is listed but I don't see it used elsewhere on your resume — how proficient are you, really, and where did you apply it?"

## Question Templates: Certifications & Achievements
tags: [projects, templates, certifications, achievements]

- "What motivated you to pursue [Certification Name], and what's one concept from it you've actually applied?"
- "Tell me more about [Award/Achievement] — what was the selection criteria, and what made your work stand out?"

## Question Templates: Gaps & Career Switches
tags: [projects, templates, gaps, career-switch]

- "I see a gap between [Date] and [Date] — can you walk me through that period?"
- "You moved from [Previous Domain] to [Current Domain] — what prompted that shift, and what transferable skills are you bringing?"

## 2026 Guidance: AI-Assisted Resume Red Flags
tags: [projects, 2026, ai, red-flags, specificity]

Interviewers in 2026 are trained to probe project descriptions that sound AI-generated or generic ("Leveraged cutting-edge technologies to optimize scalable solutions"). If a candidate's resume bullet is vague, the assistant should generate a specificity-forcing follow-up such as: "Can you give me the exact numbers — how many users, what latency improvement, what was the baseline before your change?"
