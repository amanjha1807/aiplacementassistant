# 2026 Industry Hiring Trends

## Summary
tags: [trends, 2026, summary]

A concise reference for how hiring has evolved, useful as retrieval context for any question about "what's different this year."

- AI fluency is now a baseline expectation, not a bonus skill — interviewers across both service-based and product-based companies routinely ask how candidates use AI tools responsibly.
- Asynchronous, AI-scored video interviews are common in early screening stages at large-volume hirers (service-based companies especially), evaluating clarity, structure, and confidence alongside content.
- "Debug the AI-generated code" segments have become a standard technical-round addition, testing code-review skill rather than from-scratch generation alone.
- System design has moved earlier in the pipeline — even SDE-1/fresher-adjacent interviews at well-funded product companies and startups now include lightweight design questions.
- RAG, vector databases, LLM-serving, and agent-orchestration have joined the standard system-design question pool at AI-forward companies and AI-delivery teams within service-based companies.
- Resume specificity is scrutinized more heavily, since AI tools make it easy to write impressive-sounding but vague bullet points; interviewers are trained to probe for concrete numbers and details.
- Behavioral interviews increasingly ask about AI-tool judgment — recognizing when an AI suggestion was wrong, and how the candidate verified or corrected it.

## Process-Level Shift
tags: [trends, process, 2026]

By 2026, most large employers have folded generative-AI literacy into every stage of hiring rather than treating it as a separate skill. Online assessments increasingly use AI-proctoring with live webcam and screen monitoring, adaptive question difficulty, and plagiarism detection tuned to catch AI-generated code submissions. Many product-based companies now include at least one round that explicitly evaluates how a candidate uses AI coding assistants — not to replace thinking, but to see if the candidate can direct, verify, and debug AI-generated code responsibly. Service-based companies have compressed their traditional multi-round pipelines, replacing some technical rounds with a single AI-scored coding assessment plus a shorter human interview focused on communication, aptitude, and domain fit.
