# HR & Behavioral Question Bank — 2026

## Overview
HR and behavioral rounds are evaluated on structure, honesty, and self-awareness rather than "correct" answers. The STAR framework (Situation, Task, Action, Result) remains the standard structure for behavioral answers in 2026, with an added emphasis on a fifth element some coaches call "L" for Learning — what you would do differently.

## Self-Introduction & Motivation
tags: [hr, self-introduction, motivation]

1. Tell me about yourself.
2. Walk me through your resume.
3. Why do you want to work at this company specifically?
4. Why should we hire you over another candidate?
5. What are your short-term and long-term career goals?
6. Why are you looking to leave your current company? (for experienced candidates)

Framework guidance: Structure the self-introduction as Present → Past → Future: current role/status, one or two relevant past experiences, and why this specific role fits your future direction. Keep it under 90 seconds.

## Behavioral (STAR-Based)
tags: [hr, behavioral, star, conflict, failure, leadership]

1. Tell me about a time you disagreed with a teammate or manager. How did you handle it?
2. Describe a situation where you failed. What did you learn?
3. Tell me about a time you had to work under a tight deadline.
4. Describe a time you took initiative without being asked.
5. Tell me about a time you had to learn something completely new very quickly.
6. Describe a conflict within a team project and how it was resolved.
7. Tell me about a time you received critical feedback. How did you respond?

Framework guidance: Keep each STAR answer to 4–6 sentences: 1 sentence Situation, 1 sentence Task, 2–3 sentences Action (this should be the majority of the answer), 1 sentence Result with a measurable or observable outcome.

## Situational / Hypothetical
tags: [hr, situational, hypothetical]

1. If your manager assigns you a task you disagree with, what would you do?
2. How would you handle a situation where two teammates are not cooperating with each other?
3. If you found a critical bug right before a release deadline, what would you do?
4. How would you prioritize if you were given three urgent tasks at once?

## Company & Culture Fit
tags: [hr, culture-fit, 2026, ai-judgment]

1. What do you know about our company's recent products or initiatives?
2. How do you align with our company values? (Research the specific values — e.g., Amazon's Leadership Principles, Meta's core values.)
3. Are you comfortable working in a hybrid/remote setup?
4. How do you stay updated with technology trends?
5. [2026] How do you currently use AI tools (ChatGPT, Copilot, Claude, etc.) in your work or studies, and how do you make sure you're not over-relying on them?
6. [2026] Tell me about a time an AI tool gave you an incorrect or misleading suggestion — how did you catch it?

## Salary, Logistics & Closing Questions
tags: [hr, salary, logistics, closing]

1. What are your salary expectations?
2. What is your notice period? (for experienced candidates)
3. Are you open to relocation?
4. Do you have any offers from other companies?
5. Do you have any questions for us? (Always prepare 2–3 thoughtful questions — about team structure, growth path, or current projects.)

## Answer-Quality Checklist
tags: [hr, guidance, checklist]

A strong HR answer is specific (names a real project or event), honest about weaknesses without being self-defeating, concise (avoids rambling beyond 60–90 seconds), and ends with a takeaway or result rather than trailing off.
